Creative Sprint:
The Almanac

Ten ad concepts, a playable, a video ad, and a testing plan for Cortex Studio's daily puzzle game. Built from real player language found in App Store reviews, designed to be shipped fast, killed fast, and iterated.

UNSOLICITED CONCEPT WORK BY KEELIN McLOUGHLIN · NOT AFFILIATED WITH VOODOO / CORTEX

Step 1 · Listen before making

Every creative angle below comes from something real players actually said in reviews, or from how the app positions itself (Health & Fitness, not Games: it sells self-improvement, not entertainment).

"Stop wasting time on mindless scrolling"
The store listing leads with anti-brain-rot. → C-01, C-02: guilt-relief and time-reframe hooks.
"It has even replaced my NYT daily puzzle time"
Players migrate from established puzzle rituals. → C-03: habit-transfer hook aimed at existing puzzle people.
"I've lost my growing streak numerous times… as of right now I have only a streak of 4"
Players are emotionally invested in streaks. → C-04: streak pride / loss-aversion hook.
"Ahhh pipes, especially the hard mode. I was in love."
A vocal segment wants difficulty. → C-05: ego-bait challenge hook; P-01: playable that lets them prove it.
"Not the typical cash-grab slop… clean and responsive UI"
Minimalism is a differentiator in a loud category. → C-06: anti-noise positioning against typical mobile-game ads.
"Calming and stress relieving… solving small puzzles while listening to something"
The game lives inside daily routines. → C-07: morning-ritual hook; C-08: the "aha" identity hook.
"All the games are so fun and not too easy or hard, so it constantly makes you want to play more"
Approachable difficulty is the draw, so show it: teach the whole rule inside the ad. → C-10: learn-it-in-3-seconds hook.
Patch notes: "irregular grid layouts to Shikaku levels" · "tactical hints for… Shikaku" — and Cortex's next title is literally named Shikaku
The studio keeps investing in this game, and its colored rectangles are the most visual asset in the catalog. → C-09: oddly-satisfying completion hook.

Step 2 · Ship ten rough ones

Ten statics in a shared visual system (almanac paper, ink, one accent, with Shikaku providing the color), each testing one hypothesis. Rough on purpose: the goal is signal, not polish.

C-01 · GUILT / SELF-IMPROVEMENT

brain rot, meet brain time

Hypothesis: doomscroll guilt converts better than puzzle appeal. Audience: broad 18–34. Watch: hook rate, CPI.

C-02 · TIME REFRAME

5 minutes. 0 regrets.

Hypothesis: a low time-commitment promise lowers install friction vs. C-01's guilt angle. Audience: broad. Watch: CPI vs C-01, D1 retention of installs.

C-03 · HABIT TRANSFER

finished the crossword by 9am?

Hypothesis: existing puzzle-ritual players are the cheapest high-LTV installs. Audience: word/logic game interest. Watch: CPI, D7 retention, subscription rate.

C-04 · STREAK / LOSS AVERSION

day 47

Hypothesis: showing the streak mechanic pre-install attracts habit-driven players. Audience: broad + Duolingo-style app interest. Watch: D7/D30 retention.

C-05 · CHALLENGE / EGO BAIT

92% quit this on expert

Hypothesis: classic challenge bait still prints in puzzle UA, and expert mode makes it true to the product. Audience: puzzle + strategy gamers. Watch: IPM, early churn (bait risk).

C-06 · CALM / ANTI-NOISE

no ads screaming

Hypothesis: positioning against loud mobile-game ads wins the audience most allergic to them. Audience: 30+, wellness-adjacent. Watch: CPI, payer rate (Zen Mode).

C-07 · MORNING RITUAL

coffee. almanac. then the world.

Hypothesis: anchoring to an existing ritual (coffee) beats abstract benefit claims. Audience: broad, morning dayparting. Watch: hook rate by hour of day.

C-08 · IDENTITY / BRAND

that little rush when it clicks

Hypothesis: selling the feeling (competence) outperforms selling the features. Audience: broad. Watch: CPI, creative fatigue curve.

C-09 · ODDLY SATISFYING (SHIKAKU)

some people count sheep

Hypothesis: a fully solved, colorful Shikaku board is thumb-stopping in a way monochrome statics aren't; completion imagery converts the "oddly satisfying" audience. Grounded in the studio's own bet: Shikaku gets steady patch-note investment and named their next title. Audience: broad, satisfying-content affinity. Watch: hook rate vs C-01/C-02.

C-10 · LEARN IT IN 3 SECONDS (SHIKAKU)

that's it. that's the game.

Hypothesis: showing the rule mid-solve teaches the game inside the ad, and comprehension drives installs. Grounded in the review "not too easy or hard, so it constantly makes you want to play more": approachability is the product's own pitch. Audience: broad + puzzle interest. Watch: IPM, tutorial completion of installs.

P-01 · The playable

A 15-second Pipes teaser: rotate the tiles to connect the water, with live green feedback and a time-stamped end card. Playables let the "hard mode" audience prove themselves before installing.

V-01 · The fail video

A 15-second "so close" video: the player fills the whole board, hovers over the winning tile, then taps the wrong one and the pipeline collapses. Frustration-bait that makes viewers install just to prove they'd have finished it.

Step 3 · Test, read, kill, iterate

How I would run this batch on Meta and AppLovin as a first sprint, with kill criteria agreed before launch so decisions are fast and unemotional.

StageSetupDecision rule
Week 1 · signalAll 10 statics, one broad ad set per network, equal budget, App Store CPP variants matched to C-01/C-03/C-05 anglesKill anything below the batch's median IPM after ~2k impressions each; promote top 3 by CPI
Week 2 · iterate3 winners × 3 variations each (new headline, new visual, new format: static → 15s motion cut in CapCut)Variation beats parent CPI by 10%+ or dies; playable enters test against best static
Week 3 · qualityWinning angle gets retention read: D1/D7 by creative, payer rate for Zen ModeCheap installs that don't retain get down-weighted; the metric that matters is cost per retained player, not CPI alone
Why hypotheses, not vibes: every creative above tests exactly one variable (guilt vs time vs ritual vs challenge), so a winner tells us something reusable about the audience, not just "this image worked."
Creative volume plan: this system (paper, ink, one accent, one grid motif) is deliberately templated so 10 more variants can ship in an afternoon with AI tools + CapCut, which is the actual job.
ASO tie-in: the winning ad angle should flow back into the App Store subtitle and first two screenshots, so the listing confirms whatever promise got the tap.
